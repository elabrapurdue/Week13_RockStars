Rock Stars

Jerome John Garcia

(August 1, 1942 - August 9, 1995) was an iconic American musician, best known as the lead guitarist and vocalist of the Grateful Dead, a band that became a cornerstone of the counterculture movement of the 1960s. Garcia's distinctive guitar style and improvisational skills were central to the Dead's unique blend of rock, folk, bluegrass, blues, and jazz. Beyond his work with the Grateful Dead, Garcia also collaborated on various side projects, solidifying his legacy as one of the most influential and beloved figures in rock history.

Joan Jett

(born September 22, 1958) is an American singer, guitarist, songwriter, and producer. Often referred to as the "Queen of Rock 'n' Roll," she is best known for her work with Joan Jett & the Blackhearts, including hits like "I Love Rock 'n' Roll" and "Bad Reputation". A founding member of The Runaways, Jett has been a trailblazer for women in rock music, influencing countless artists.

Freddie Mercury

(September 5, 1946 – November 24, 1991) was a British singer, songwriter, and the charismatic frontman of the legendary rock band Queen. Renowned for his powerful vocals and electrifying stage presence, Mercury wrote iconic songs like "Bohemian Rhapsody" and "Somebody to Love". He is celebrated as one of the greatest rock performers of all time.

Kurt Cobain

(February 20, 1967 – April 5, 1994) was an American singer, guitarist, and songwriter, best known as the frontman of the grunge band Nirvana. A voice of Generation X, Cobain penned anthems like "Smells Like Teen Spirit" and "Come as You Are." His introspective lyrics and raw sound helped define the grunge movement of the early 1990s.

Prince

(June 7, 1958 – April 21, 2016) was an American singer, songwriter, multi-instrumentalist, and record producer. Known for his eclectic style and flamboyant stage presence, Prince was a musical innovator whose hits include "Purple Rain," "When Doves Cry," and "Kiss." He blended funk, rock, R&B, and pop to create a sound uniquely his own.
